Panayapatti Inauguration Event

The Compute Initiative (TCi) is proud to announce the inauguration of a new AI-enabled computer lab, a community initiative led by its founders, Anish Subramanian and Anuj Subramanian.
The inauguration event took place on Friday, October 3, 2025, at 9:00 AM at the Govt. Higher Secondary School, in Panayapatti (Pudukottai District).
We are honored to have had the Honorable Tamil Nadu Minister for Natural Resources, Mr. S. Ragupathi, officiate the ceremony. He was joined by local educators, dignitaries, and community leaders.

This initiative aims to empower rural students by introducing Computer Science as a standard subject for grades 6 through 10. TCi will donate computers and provide ongoing support with a dedicated curriculum and teaching staff, enabling students to gain vital skills in modern technology, including resposible AI learning through a strong collaboration with local volunteers and educators.

Student Recognition Ceremony

The Co-Founders and Administrative Team for The Compute Initiative , Anish Subramanian and Anuj Subramanian and the SRM Arunachalam Staff, were honored to attend the Student Recognition Ceremony at SRM Arunachalam Higher Secondary School in Panayapatti on December 24, 2025. This special event celebrated the achievements of students who have excelled in their computer science studies since the launch of our first computer lab in October 2025. It was inspiring to see the dedication and hard work of these students being recognized by their school and community. We are proud to support their educational journey and look forward to seeing all they will accomplish in the future.

Second School Opens — Kuruvikondanpatti

In 2026, The Compute Initiative installed its second computer lab at a government-aided school in Kuruvikondanpatti, extending the same daily computer science curriculum that launched at Panayapatti to a new community of students. With this expansion, TCI now runs a daily curriculum across 2 schools, has delivered 700+ teaching hours to 200 students, and is supported by our newest teacher, Saranya.

TCI is Now an Official Code Club

The Compute Initiative is officially registered as a Code Club, part of the Raspberry Pi Foundation's global network of free coding clubs for young people. Our students now have the opportunity to learn to code games, animations, and websites, develop critical-thinking and problem-solving skills, and share their knowledge with others — with Anish and Anuj Subramanian recognized as Code Club Leader and Mentor.

THE COMPUTE INITIATIVE

Talent is everywhere, but opportunity isn’t ... yet. In the  villages of Tamil Nadu, bright high school students are  being left behind in the digital age.

The Compute Initiative works to close this gap by providing computers, internet access, and guidance to students unfamiliar with these resources.
