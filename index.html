Who We Are

The Compute Initiative (TCi) was founded by Anish and Anuj Subramanian with a simple belief: where you grow up shouldn't determine what you can achieve.

We partner with schools in rural Tamil Nadu, including SRM Arunachalam Higher Secondary School in Panayapatti and Thamarai Schools International, to bring computers, trained teachers, and real digital skills into classrooms that have gone without for too long. Together with dedicated local educators, we're making computer education a part of everyday learning.

What We Do
The Challenge
  • Millions of students in rural India are growing up without access to the tools that define the modern world.
  • In many nagarathar schools, there are no computers, no trained tech educators, and no exposure to the skills] that today's workforce demands.
  • Without intervention, these students will be left behind before they even get a chance to compete.
Your Impact
  • Directly help us equip classrooms with computers, software, and internet access with donations
  • Hire and train dedicated teachers in digital literacy and AI
  • Show students that the future is something they can build.
Success Story
  • Today, 80 students in grades 6–9 are learning basic programming in a fully equipped lab that didn't exist a year ago. That's 80 kids who now know what it feels like to write their first line of code.
  • And we're just getting started: 10 more schools have been identified for our next phase.
Faces of Change

Behind every statistic is a student seeing a computer for the first time, a teacher sharing a passion for coding, a parent watching their child's oppurtunities and capabilities expand. These are the people driving The Compute Initiative forward and with your support, there will be many more.

THE COMPUTE INITIATIVE

Talent is everywhere, but opportunity isn’t ... yet. In the  villages of Tamil Nadu, bright high school students are  being left behind in the digital age.

The Compute Initiative works to close this gap by providing computers, internet access, and guidance to students unfamiliar with these resources.
